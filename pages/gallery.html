And so what?

In the past women who smoked were viewed as ‘fallen women’. So widespread was the social stigma attached to smoking that as late as 1908 a woman in New York was arrested for smoking in the street. By 1929 the tobacco industry capitalised on changing attitudes toward women and promoted smoking as a symbol of emancipation, a ‘torch of freedom’.
